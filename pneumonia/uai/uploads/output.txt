**Pneumonia: General Information**

* **What is it?** Pneumonia is an infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs.  The air sacs may fill with fluid or
 pus (purulent material), causing cough with phlegm or pus, fever, chills, and difficulty breathing.  Many types of organisms can cause pneumonia, including bacteria, viruses, and fungi.

* **Symptoms:**  Common symptoms include:
    * Cough (may produce phlegm)
    *
 Fever, chills, and sweats
    * Shortness of breath
    * Chest pain
    * Fatigue
    * Nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea (in some cases)

* **Risk Factors:**  Certain factors increase the risk of developing pneumonia, including:
    * Older age
    * Weaken
ed immune system
    * Chronic lung conditions (e.g., asthma, COPD)
    * Smoking
    * Heart failure
    * Recent respiratory viral infection

* **Treatment:** Treatment depends on the cause of the pneumonia and may include antibiotics (for bacterial pneumonia), antiviral medications (for viral pneumonia), antifungal
 medications (for fungal pneumonia), rest, fluids, and supportive care.  Severe cases may require hospitalization and oxygen therapy.


**If you are experiencing symptoms suggestive of pneumonia, please seek immediate medical attention.**  This is not an exhaustive list of symptoms or risk factors, and a proper medical evaluation is crucial for diagnosis and
 treatment.
